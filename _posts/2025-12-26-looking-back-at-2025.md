The years seem to accelerate, and 2025 was a year of stark contrasts. Looking back at my posts from 2018 and 2019, I wrote a lot about "optimization." Back then, I was optimizing for growth and entry into the industry. In 2025, the stakes changed. This year wasn't just about my output; it was about the weight of my roles—as a leader, a husband, and a father.

It has been a year of hard-won clarity (or is there? :p), often found through friction.

#### Health: The System Crash
This was the year I learned that you cannot "vibe code" your way out of physical neglect. 
In the first half of the year, work demands eroded my gym and running habits. 
When I tried to force a comeback in August, I pushed a cold engine too hard. 

A back injury in October was followed by a bad fall in November and a 
recurrence of an unknown, yet stubborn skin rash. I spent years 
thinking of health as a variable I could dial up or down. 
I was wrong. It is the hardware that runs the software; without it, the code doesn't matter.

#### Family: Strength in My Absence
The toll of work wasn't just on my body; it was on my presence. 
It wasn't just that I was exhausted; it was that I simply wasn't there enough. 
Work took its pound of flesh, and I found myself missing the small, everyday moments with my children.

However, they’ve shown they are strong in their own right. 
They’ve been coping with their schoolwork and navigating their challenges; 
while there’s definitely room for improvement, I’m proud of their resilience. 
I am learning that success is a multi-threaded process. 
Being a leader at work is a different "process" than being a father or a husband. 
Each role deserves its own fair treatment and dedicated focus.

#### Wealth & Tech: The Automation Win
In the middle of the chaos, I had a significant technical breakthrough. 
Using the "vibe coding" workflow that has defined 2025, 
I finally automated my financial expenses. 
I built a system that automatically ingests spendings from PayNow, PayLah, and all my credit cards. 

It uses AI to analyze and categorize expenses. 
Vibe coding also quickly put together a prettier dashboard than Excel.
It’s not perfect—it gets the "vibe" wrong occasionally—but it has 
removed the manual friction that used to kill my tracking habits. 
It’s a win for my "Wealth" pillar that finally feels 21st-century.

#### 2026 Strategy: Rehabilitation and Presence
The "marathon" of 2025 was more of a crawl, but the direction remains the same. 

1. **Rehabilitation:** Physical therapy is the priority. No more "crunching" my physical recovery.
2. **Presence:** Shifting from "quantity of time" to "quality of presence" with my family.
3. **Compartmentalization:** Giving each role its best and fair treatment.

As I wrote years ago: the goal is to be better today than I was yesterday. 
Sometimes, "better" just means being more present for the people who matter most.
